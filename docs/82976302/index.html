Warden playbook

◆
Warden is a durable frontline initiator who wins by locking one target with Flask (stamina drain) into Binding Word, then out-DPSing them while Willpower and Last Stand keep him alive. His entire value is surviving on the frontline, so weapon damage WITHOUT armour — exactly your 7/2/3 split this game — is the classic Warden mistake. You got away with it because your team snowballed (+84,955 souls), not because the build was sound.
Situational play
Laning vs a poke/range duo
Against Apollo + Mirage you took 5,332 to 3,800 in lane — buy High-Velocity Rounds first to actually trade, and don't sit in their crossfire without a sustain item; your 4:25 death came from exactly that.
Picking off a fed carry
Your three mid kills on Apollo show the pattern works — Flask to drain his dash, Binding Word to root, then burst. Aim it at the higher-net-worth target (Mirage at 32k was the one you under-damaged).
Committing Last Stand
You took 19,655 damage in the Late phase and survived via 7,837 self-heal — Last Stand is doing work, but its windup is interruptible, so pop it from cover and consider Unstoppable into heavy-CC enemies.
Deep deaths
Your 27:03 death cost 59 seconds dead at -6830,-3427, deep on the enemy side. When ahead, take that farm with vision and an escape (Metal Skin / Enduring Speed) rather than fighting outnumbered.
Macro: turning fights into objectives
  • The game was won on objectives, not your KDA: team soul lead went from +677 at 30:00 to +84,955 after the 31:31 mid-boss — keep prioritising the mid-boss as the lead-snowballing objective, exactly as your team did here.
  • Your early objective tempo was good (lane-1 tier-1 down at 14:18, team ahead +5,002 by 15:00). Convert those leads into the next tier rather than farming jungle when the map is open.
  • Three of your four deaths were deep on the enemy half (around -6900 x). When you're the frontline and ahead, push with the team and vision instead of soloing into the Apollo/Mirage side.
  • You carried the urn/objective role well as a durable Warden — keep being the body that absorbs first, but buy the armour that lets that body survive the fights it starts.
