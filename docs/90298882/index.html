Warden playbook

◆
Warden is the tankiest frontline initiator in the game when built correctly — a peeler who locks one squishy with Flask plus Binding Word and deletes them, while Last Stand lets him brawl through fights with bonus resist. This match you played the offence (10 kills, strong lockdown on Geist and Infernus) but skipped the defence that makes the kit work: an 8/2/2 split with no armour meant you took more damage than you dealt in all four phases and died 5 times before 16 minutes. Win condition for your Warden: survive the frontline so your CC keeps landing.

Losing the lane trade
You died 3x to Infernus in lane taking 6,154 vs 3,951 — when trades go negative, lead with Flask to cut his weapon damage and drain his stamina, then disengage rather than brawling armourless.
Heavy spirit enemy team
Lady Geist did 40,000 to you and Infernus is spirit-built too — Improved Spirit Armour and Diviner's Kevlar by mid game are mandatory, not optional, against this lineup.
Considering a self-damage item
Blood Tribute gave you 29,314 self-damage with no armour to absorb it — only take sacrifice items once you have real mitigation; otherwise pick Siphon Bullets or Vampiric Burst for sustain without the self-harm.
Mid-to-late teamfights
You went 0 deaths in Mid and racked 11 assists in Late — that's the Warden pattern working: open from cover with Last Stand, trap multiple enemies, and let the team follow your Binding Word root.
