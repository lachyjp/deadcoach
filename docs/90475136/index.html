Warden playbook

◆
Warden is a durable frontline initiator whose whole win condition is surviving on the front line long enough to lock one target down — Flask to drain stamina (removing their dash), Binding Word to root, then bonus bullet damage to delete them while Last Stand tanks the return fire. This match is the proof by absence: you had the damage (top in the game) but built like a backline carry, so the moment a fight lasted more than a few seconds you died. Played to type, your 41,451 into Vyper becomes kills you actually walk away from.
Situational play
Laning a losing trade
When a trade turns against you (like the 4:46 death to Vyper), Flask to cut their weapon damage and walk, don't commit. Warden wins extended trades only with Willpower up and some armour — without it, disengage and farm safe.
Teamfights (your bread and butter)
Open with Last Stand from cover so the windup can't be interrupted, then Flask+Binding Word the enemy carry. Don't start the fight as the first one caught — your two Dynamo deaths came from being CC'd before you could channel.
Getting bursted by spirit (Celeste, Lady Geist)
The two sub-8-second Celeste deaths were pure spirit burst. Pop Willpower (it scales with Spirit Power) pre-emptively and itemise Diviner's Kevlar / spirit armour so you live through the combo and turn it around.
Ahead and grouping
When your team has tempo (you took both mid-bosses), front for them on objectives instead of farming a side lane. A live Warden initiating is worth far more than the extra 1,500 souls you chased into those late deaths.
Macro: turning fights into objectives
  • Your team won both mid-bosses (27:05 and 37:14) — that buff window is your cue to force the next objective immediately, not reset to farm. Convert the man-advantage from the buff into a Walker.
  • Five of your eight deaths were late and ~80 seconds dead each. At that respawn timer, treat every late fight as 'do not die first' — group up and front, because a single bad death is a full minute of 5v6 for your team.
  • Stop farming the far side lane when objectives are live; the souls you gained late (1,504/min) were partly cancelled by feeding picks. Push your wave in first, then rotate with the team — never rotate into a fight with a wave bouncing back at your own base.
  • Use Warden's mid-game mobility to be the urn/objective carrier and the one who starts mid-boss with Last Stand — that's higher value than chasing the scoreboard for damage.
